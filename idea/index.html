The Mission
Dreams to Wings Project

The Dreams to Wings project aims to recreate the historic Vickers Vimy aircraft that completed the first flight from England to Australia in 1919. This ambitious project involves detailed engineering, international coordination, specialized manufacturing, and a commemorative journey following the original route.

This website provides a comprehensive breakdown of the project, from concept to execution, along with a detailed strategy plan, project checklist, and visual elements to guide implementation.

Phase 1: Research & Planning
First project phase

This phase involves gathering all historical documentation, photographs, and technical specifications of the original Vickers Vimy aircraft. The team conducts interviews with aviation historians and experts to ensure historical accuracy.

Key activities include:

  • Historical document collection
  • Expert interviews
  • Route planning
  • Initial budget estimation
  • Team assembly
Phase 2: Engineering & Design
Second project phase

During this phase, the engineering team creates detailed blueprints and 3D models of the aircraft based on the historical documentation. Modern engineering principles are applied while maintaining historical accuracy.

Key activities include:

  • Creating detailed blueprints
  • 3D modeling of all components
  • Structural analysis
  • Material selection
  • Safety modifications
Phase 3: Manufacturing
Third project phase

The manufacturing phase involves the fabrication and assembly of all aircraft components according to the engineering specifications. Specialized manufacturing partners are engaged for various components.

Key activities include:

  • Component fabrication
  • Engine assembly
  • Airframe construction
  • Systems integration
  • Quality control
Phase 4: Testing
Fourth project phase

The testing phase ensures the aircraft meets all safety and performance requirements. This includes ground testing, initial flight tests, and certification processes with aviation authorities.

Key activities include:

  • Ground testing
  • Initial flight tests
  • Performance optimization
  • Regulatory certification
  • Pilot training
Phase 5: Journey
Final project phase

The journey phase is the culmination of the project, where the recreated Vickers Vimy aircraft follows the historic route from England to Australia, retracing the steps of the original 1919 flight.

Key activities include:

  • Route preparation
  • Support team coordination
  • Media coverage
  • Public events
  • Documentation

Historical Significance
The Original Flight
The 1919 England to Australia flight by the Vickers Vimy, piloted by Sir Ross and Sir Keith Smith, was a pioneering achievement in aviation history, covering over 11,000 miles in 28 days.
Engineering Marvel
The Vickers Vimy was a British heavy bomber aircraft that represented cutting-edge technology for its time, with a wingspan of over 67 feet and powered by two Rolls-Royce Eagle VIII engines.
Legacy
This recreation project honors their legacy and brings to life an important piece of aviation history for future generations to appreciate and learn from.
